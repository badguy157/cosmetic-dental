Teeth Whitening

Make your teeth bright and white. Safe and quick. Good for coffee or tea lovers who want a fresh look.

Who teeth whitening is for

  • Your teeth are stained from coffee, tea, wine, or smoking
  • You want a brighter smile for a special event
  • You have healthy teeth and gums (no cavities or sensitivity issues)
  • You're looking for a quick, non-invasive treatment
  • You want to look more youthful and refreshed

The process

1

Consultation

We check your teeth and gums to make sure whitening is safe for you. We'll discuss your goals and show you shade options.

2

Choose your method

Pick between in-office whitening (fast results in one visit) or take-home trays (gradual whitening over 1-2 weeks).

3

Treatment

For in-office: we apply professional-strength gel and activate it with a special light. For take-home: we create custom trays and give you whitening gel to use at home.

4

Enjoy your results

Your teeth will be noticeably brighter! We'll give you tips on keeping them white and offer touch-up treatments if needed.

Common questions

Will whitening damage my teeth?

No. Professional whitening is safe when done correctly. We use proven methods that don't harm your enamel. Some people get temporary sensitivity, but it goes away within a day or two.

How white will my teeth get?

Most people see their teeth get 3-8 shades whiter. Results depend on your starting shade and the type of stains you have. We'll give you realistic expectations during your consultation.

How long do the results last?

With good care, results can last 1-3 years. Avoid staining foods and drinks (coffee, red wine, dark berries), don't smoke, and brush regularly. Touch-ups every 6-12 months can maintain your brightness.

Can I whiten if I have crowns or fillings?

Whitening only works on natural teeth. If you have visible crowns or fillings, they'll stay the same color. We can discuss replacing them to match your newly whitened teeth if needed.

How much does teeth whitening cost?

In-office whitening and take-home kits have different price points. We'll explain both options and costs at your consultation. See our pricing page for general ranges.
